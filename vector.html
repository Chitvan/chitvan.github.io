An artist's block

I picked up Illustrator in a Graphics Design class. My first experience was jumping right in without any tutorials, experimenting with the tools and generating a final result for the projects then. I'd say I learned the software the hard way by taking a difficult approach of scanning an artwork and adjusting vector points. Features like tracing a shape are the best part of illustrator for me and it took me a while to discover those.

For this project, it was difficult to come with an idea. I kept brainstorming and never really reached a strong visual. So I started looking at various sites for inspiration. While browsing through Unsplash website, I came across the image with the two jars in background. I liked it and wanted to use it in some way. From there, the rest of the idea came together. The character I drew came naturally as it's is a comic book character I've drawn for years.

In making this project I explored various Illustrator tools. The text labels in black was a bit of experimentation with rounded corners. I used a similar approach with the fluid in Motivation jar. I sketched the pose of character on paper first but when I started using the pen tool in Illustrator, the lines didn't come out to be smooth. So I redid it a bunch of times.

Overall, I am happy how I turned my "no ideas" phase into an idea! I really like working in Illustrator.
